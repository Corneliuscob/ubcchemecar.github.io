HELLO.

WE ARE UBC CHEM-E-CAR.

A UBC Engineering Design Team.

Transforming Energy

We specialize in safely transforming potential energy stored in chemicals to mechanical energy with accurate control of the reactions involved.

What We Do

We are a team of UBC undergraduate students committed to building the best and most accurate Chem-E-Car ever, emphasizing sustainability and clean energy production. We design, test and build a shoe-box sized car from scratch, from selecting the right chemical reactions all the way to building the circuitry and assembling the mechanical components. We make sure every single component fits perfectly.

UBC Chem-E-Car is one of the few engineering design teams in UBC that represent chemical engineers. However, we appreciate diversity and do have members from other engineering disciplines and the humanities on board.

Fun fact: we have team members from 5 continents with the exception of Antartica and Australia.

AIChE

We compete in the annual American Institute of Chemical Engineers (AIChE) Chem-E-Car competition with other North American teams.

The AIChE Chem-E-Car Competition

The Car

The competition involves making a shoe-box sized car below $2000 that travels a certain distance with a given cargo in under 2 minutes.

The Chemistry

To complicate the otherwise trivial task, the car’s stopping mechanism must be chemical – no physical or electronic clocks can be used for stopping.

The Challenge

We are only given the cargo and travel distance 1 hour ahead of the competition. Extensive knowledge of the chemistry involved is essential for accurate control.

We need your help!

UBC Chem-E-Car provides invaluable technical and educational experiences for all UBC undergraduate students. Our former team members have landed exciting careers and internships using the skills gained in UBC Chem-E-Car. Your generous support helps create more opportunities for all UBC students.
