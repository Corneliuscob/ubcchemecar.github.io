Our Sponsors

We'd like to thank the following organizations for their kind support and various contributions, without which this project would not have been possible.

UBC Department of Chemical and Biological Engineering

The Department provides workshop services and lab space for the team to conduct experiments safely and has been very supportive of all Chem-E-Car activities.

Precision Nanosystems

Precision Nanosystems is a frontrunner in the development of nanomedicines and genetic modelling. Their technology allows for research to be performed 20X faster than the gold standard systems while also being 10X less expensive.

Solidworks

Solidworks has kindly provided UBC Chem-E-Car with CAD software for all our designing and modelling needs.

The University of British Columbia

UBC Chem-E-Car is funded by The Faculty of Applied Science Professional Activities Fund (PAF).

Shell Canada Experiential Learning Fund

The Shell Experiential Learning Fund (SELF) provides funding for University of British Columbia students to participate in enhanced educational experiences. Funding priority is placed on projects related to the Oil and Gas industry and in the Faculties of Engineering and Science (Geosciences) and School of Business.

DigitalOcean

As described in their website, DigitalOcean is a cloud infrastructure provider focused on simplifying web infrastructure for software developers. They have kindly provided support and funding for our hosting and software development needs.

Free Geek Vancouver

Free Geek is a nonprofit community organization that reduces the environmental impact of waste electronics by reusing and recycling donated technology. Through community engagement they provide education, job skills training, Internet access and free or low cost computers to the public. If you're in Vancouver, please consider volunteering with them. Free Geek has donated 2 computers to UBC Chem-E-Car as part of a Hardware Grant for non-profit organizations.

Cabot

Cabot Corporation is a leading global specialty chemicals and performance materials company headquartered in Boston, Massachusetts, USA. Cabot has kindly provided the team a free sample of Vulcan XC-72 high surface area carbon black for our projects.
